Peak weekly volume target: approximately 60 km per week with the four-session layout. Composition to reach ~60 km at peak: one long run of 30–32 km, two quality sessions (intervals + tempo) totaling moderate mileage, and one easy/recovery run of ~8–10 km. Example peak-week breakdown: Saturday long run 30–32 km, Tuesday intervals session including warm-up and cool-down ~8–12 km total, Thursday tempo session 12–16 km, Sunday easy 8–10 km — combined this reaches roughly 60 km. During the specific marathon block most weeks will intentionally be slightly lower (around 50–58 km) to avoid overstressing the body given only four running sessions. Weekly progression will be gradual from the current baseline (~40 km) toward this peak, using structured increases and cutback weeks. The goal is to prioritize quality and marathon-specific adaptation rather than very high frequency; with only four runs per week, long runs become the primary mileage driver and need planned progression and recovery.
