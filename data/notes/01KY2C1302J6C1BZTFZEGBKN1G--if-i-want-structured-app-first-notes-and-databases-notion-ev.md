title: 'If I want structured, app-first notes and databases: Notion, Evernote, OneNote'
type: idea
---

If I value built-in structure—rich blocks, templates, relational databases, kanban boards, and collaboration—an app-first system like Notion, Evernote, or OneNote is the fastest route. Notion excels at databases, templates, and team collaboration; it's great for project systems and mixed media pages, but has limited E2EE and offline capabilities compared with file-based setups. Evernote offers a polished note UI, strong search, and clipping tools; it is proprietary and best if I want a single unified note app. OneNote (paired with OneDrive) is free, integrates tightly with Microsoft Office, and is good for freeform notes and stylus input. Trade-offs: app-first systems provide fast features and collaboration but sacrifice portability (content often lives in proprietary formats) and often lack true E2EE. If my workflow depends on relational databases, templates, or sharing with others, pick Notion or Google Workspace; if I prioritize long-term exportability and local access, consider a file-based approach instead.
