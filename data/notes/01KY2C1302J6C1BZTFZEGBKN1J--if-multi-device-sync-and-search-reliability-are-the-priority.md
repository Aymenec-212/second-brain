title: 'If multi-device sync and search/reliability are the priority: Google Drive
  or Dropbox'
type: idea
---

If my highest priority is flawless multi-device sync, fast global search, and low maintenance, Google Drive (and Google Workspace) or Dropbox are the most pragmatic choices. Google Drive provides 15 GB free per account, excellent full-text search across documents, and tight integration with Google Docs/Sheets/Keep. It is highly cross-platform (web, Android, iOS, Windows, macOS, Linux via third-party clients) and scales well for both personal and team use. Dropbox is a classic file-sync service with a reliable client, version history, and broad third-party support; it's particularly good for syncing plain files (Markdown) while preserving folder structure across every device. Both services are provider-managed (not E2EE) but offer easy sharing, collaboration, and uptime guarantees. Use these if search and “it just works” reliability across many OSes is more important than strict privacy or E2EE. If I need full-text search over notes stored as Markdown, Google Drive+Docs or indexing via a local app plus Dropbox often gives the best user experience.
