Sam, 31, backend/ML engineer at a Paris logistics startup. Quick history of personal experiments with note systems: Evernote (2016–2019), Roam trial (2020), heavy Notion setup (2021), Obsidian experiments (2022), plain Markdown + git (2023). Pattern: initial enthusiasm for 1–3 weeks, then growing friction (mobile capture slow, too many templates, tags explode). The backlog grows and I stop doing the weekly review. Concrete current state and metrics: Inbox ≈ 482 notes (mostly meeting bullets, half-baked ideas, recipes, family todo). In 2025 I completed the weekly review only 4 times. Average processing time per note has been >5 minutes because I over-structure everything. My real goals for a working system are explicit: capture ideas quickly; keep project next-actions visible; store searchable code snippets; meeting notes that link to tasks; and a small reference for family finances/taxes/recipes. I suspect the main causes of failure: no single committed capture point, trying to combine too many methodologies at once (GTD + PARA + Zettelkasten), and vague or missing review processes. Decision: run a small, strict 30-day experiment to validate a minimal routine (start 2026-02-10, end 2026-03-11) and surface blind spots.
