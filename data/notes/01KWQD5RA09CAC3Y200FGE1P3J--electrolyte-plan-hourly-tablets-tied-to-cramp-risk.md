Electrolyte strategy: take one electrolyte tablet every hour during the run, timed at 0, 60 and 120 minutes (i.e., at the start and then each full hour). This is motivated by a personal tendency toward mild cramping when electrolytes are low. Taking an electrolyte tablet hourly should mitigate sodium/potassium losses in sweat across a 3‑hour effort at ~25°C. Plan to carry three tablets and dissolve/take them according to the manufacturer instructions (with water). If cramps begin despite the hourly tablets, add extra salt (see fallback plan). If GI upset occurs, adjust by reducing gels first rather than skipping electrolytes, because cramps have been linked to low electrolytes previously.
