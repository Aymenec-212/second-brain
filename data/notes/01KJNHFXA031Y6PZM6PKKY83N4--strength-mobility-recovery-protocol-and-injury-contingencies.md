Strength and recovery plan: two 20-minute strength sessions per week (Monday and Friday mornings) focusing on posterior chain, glutes, and core to support running economy and reduce injury risk. Exercises should prioritize deadlift variations, hip-hinge movements, single-leg strength (split squats, step-ups), glute bridges/hip thrusts, and targeted core work. Include 10–15 minutes of mobility and foam rolling after runs as maintenance — hips, glutes, hamstrings, calves, and thoracic mobility. If minor niggles appear (e.g., hamstring irritation), immediate responses are: (a) temporarily replace one run with cross-training (bike/elliptical/pool) while maintaining strength; (b) reduce interval volume and intensity first before cutting tempo or long runs; (c) prioritize sleep, hydration, and soft-tissue work; (d) consult a physio if symptoms persist beyond a week. The plan commits to ongoing maintenance strength throughout all phases and to adjusting running load proactively rather than allowing small issues to become injuries.
