type: fact

Quick reference for storage tiers and typical costs relevant to picking a cloud for a second brain: Free tiers: iCloud offers 5 GB; Google Drive offers 15 GB; Dropbox Basic offers 2 GB; Notion has a generous free personal tier (feature-limited compared with paid plans). Paid ranges: consumer cloud storage plans (Dropbox/Google/OneDrive) typically start around $2–$10 per month depending on capacity (e.g., 100 GB up to multiple TB). Obsidian Sync is a paid Obsidian service and historically costs roughly $8–$16 per year for personal use (check current pricing). Self-hosted Nextcloud costs vary by hosting choice: a personal VPS can be $5–$10/month for small instances, or higher for managed hosting/storage. Proton Drive and other privacy-focused services have their own paid plans (often comparable to mainstream providers). When budgeting, include the cost of time/maintenance for self-hosting (server updates, backups, security) and the potential cost of premium features (E2EE, increased storage, collaboration). For a personal second brain with modest storage needs, a small paid cloud plan or Obsidian Sync is often cheaper and simpler than a self-hosted server unless privacy or customization justifies it.
