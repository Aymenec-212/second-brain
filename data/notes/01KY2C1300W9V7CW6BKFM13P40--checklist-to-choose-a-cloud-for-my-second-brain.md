I need a short decision checklist to pick the right cloud for my second brain. Before evaluating providers I must answer five core questions for my situation: 1) Do I require end-to-end encryption (E2EE) where only I hold the keys, or is provider-managed encryption (at-rest/in-transit) acceptable? If E2EE is required, that rules out many mainstream apps. 2) Which devices and OS must sync reliably: iPhone, iPad, Android, Windows, macOS, Linux? Some providers (iCloud) are best for Apple-only; others (Dropbox, Google Drive) are better cross-platform. 3) Do I prefer raw files (plain text/Markdown + attachments) that I can open/edit anywhere, or a structured app (Notion/Evernote) with built-in databases and blocks? Raw files give portability; apps give features. 4) Is collaboration/team access needed, or is this strictly personal? Team use pushes toward Notion/Google Workspace/OneDrive. 5) Budget and willingness to self-host: do I need entirely free services, modest paid tiers, or am I willing to run a Nextcloud server on a VPS? Answering these five items will narrow choices from dozens to a handful of sensible providers and sync models.
