title: Fallback rules for GI trouble and cramping during the run
type: decision
---

Predefined fallback actions if problems arise during the run: 1) If gastrointestinal (GI) upset or nausea emerges, reduce gel intake from 5 to 4 by skipping the final 2:40 gel (i.e., stop after the 2:00 gel), and rely on the banana (~90 kcal) plus the remaining gels and electrolytes to supply calories and sodium. 2) If cramping begins (muscle cramps), immediately slow pace and take extra salt (extra electrolyte tablet or concentrated salt source) in addition to continuing to sip fluids. 3) If GI upset is mild and manageable, consider spacing the next gel slightly later and taking extra water with it. 4) Test comfort in the first 30–60 minutes: if the drinks/vest/handheld feels wrong, adjust or jettison handheld early and continue with vest flasks. These rules prioritize safety and comfort over strict adherence to the fueling schedule. Record these triggers and precise actions so responses are automatic mid‑run.
