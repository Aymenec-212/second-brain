I chose running power plus RPE as the primary metrics to judge sessions while managing the knee. Rationale: running power responds immediately to changes in effort and terrain and gives a consistent external target for intensity; RPE captures the subjective knee response and overall session stress. Pace will still be used as a reference on flat, consistent routes, but it's less reliable across varied terrain and under fatigue. Heart rate is deprioritised because it lags with intensity changes and can be misleading for interval work or when trying to protect an injured structure. Practically this means: prescribe target watts and subjective effort bands for sessions; if power hardware isn’t available, use perceived effort first and pace second; log RPE and knee response after each session to inform ongoing adjustments.
