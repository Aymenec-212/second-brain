  licensing; AWS has spot/savings and many pricing tools'

Pricing is complex on both platforms; neither is categorically cheaper in all cases. Azure can be cost‑advantageous for organizations with existing Microsoft enterprise agreements or licensing commitments (Windows Server, SQL Server, Microsoft 365) because of bundled discounts and hybrid benefit programs. AWS provides extensive pricing options for cost optimization, including spot instances, reserved instances, savings plans, and detailed tooling to analyze and reduce spend. Both providers are comparable on global reliability and have extensive regional footprints, but specific regions and services vary — I should validate service availability in required regions for production workloads. For procurement and long‑term cost planning, I need to model expected workloads and check enterprise licensing terms if my target employers use Microsoft licensing extensively.
